About Yuvi

Yuvraj Singh, fondly known as Yuvi, is a name that resonates with cricket enthusiasts worldwide. Born on December 12, 1981, in Chandigarh, India, Yuvraj's journey in cricket has been nothing short of extraordinary. His natural flair for the game, combined with an indomitable spirit, propelled him to the upper echelons of cricketing glory.

Career Highlights

Yuvraj Singh's cricketing career is studded with numerous milestones and unforgettable moments. From his explosive batting to his agile fielding and deceptive bowling, Yuvraj's versatility on the field has always dazzled fans. Here are some of the highlights:

  • 2000 Under-19 World Cup: Yuvraj's talent first came to the fore during the ICC Under-19 World Cup in 2000, where he played a pivotal role in India's triumph.
  • NatWest Series Final, 2002: In one of the most memorable innings in ODI history, Yuvraj's blistering 69-ball 84 helped India chase down a daunting target against England in the NatWest Series final.
  • ICC T20 World Cup, 2007: Yuvraj's scintillating form in the inaugural T20 World Cup played a crucial role in India's victory, including his six sixes in an over against Stuart Broad.
  • ICC Cricket World Cup, 2011: A key member of the Indian team, Yuvraj's all-round performance, including crucial wickets and vital runs, played a significant role in India's triumph in the 2011 World Cup.
  • Cancer Battle and Comeback: Yuvraj's battle with cancer off the field and his triumphant return to cricket symbolize his unparalleled courage and determination.

Achievements

  1. In a 2007 ICC World Twenty20 match, he hit six sixes in a single over.
  2. He holds the record for fastest T20 fifty by scoring it in 12 balls against England national cricket team during the 2007 ICC World Twenty20
  3. He was the Man of the Tournament in the ICC Cricket World Cup 2011.
  4. He was awarded with the Arjuna Award (India's second highest, Sporting Award) in 2012 by the Prime Minister of India and President of India.
  5. In 2014, he was awarded with the Padma Shri Award.
  6. In February 2014, he was honoured with FICCI Most Inspiring Sportsperson of the Year Award.
  7. He is a two-time Indian Premier League champion. He won one championship with the Sunrisers Hyderabad in 2016 and another one with the Mumbai Indians in 2019.
